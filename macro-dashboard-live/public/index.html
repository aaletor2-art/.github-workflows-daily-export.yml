Automation Rules In This Document

Shared logic
HotInflation is high, policy rates are high, or the real-rate gap is still negative enough that credibility and currency pressure matter.
WarmPolicy is restrictive or inflation is still above target, but the next move is more balanced.
CoolInflation is subdued or policy has room to ease; watch for weak demand and currency effects.
NeutralPolicy is neither clearly hawkish nor clearly easing based on the current simplified rule set.
SpecialMonetary policy is not best represented by a simple policy-rate lens, such as Singapore's exchange-rate-band framework.
